Instructions
This experiment asks you to think about language as a transaction:  messages have costs, communication has rewards.  Your goal is to successfully indicate one of several objects to a partner. For the following questions, imagine that:
  • You must communicate with your partner by sending one of a limited number of messages. 
  • Sending a message incurs a cost.
  • Different messages cost different amounts.  
  • If your partner selects the intended object, communication is successful and you and your partner receive a reward.

In this hypothetical scenario, the three objects to communicate about are: a rose, a tulip, or a daisy. If your partner successfully understands which object you are referring to, you will both receive a reward of $${reward}.
 

There are four possible messages which can be sent, each with their own costs. These are as follows:

Message   Cost
"Rose" $${rose_cost}
"Daisy" $${daisy_cost}
"Tulip" $${tulip_cost}
"Flower" $${flower_cost}

 

Note: In each of the following questions, you will be asked to indicate your likelihood to send one of the two messages. Please indicate this by dragging the slider to reflect the strength of your preference for one message over another. For example, if you drag the slider all the way to the left, this means you would only ever send the message on the left-hand side, whereas leaving the slider in the center (between the two options) means you would be equally likely to send either of the messages.
